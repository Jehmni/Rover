## 1. What is Rover?

Rover is a transport coordination app for churches, conferences, schools, and any organisation that needs to get people to events. It replaces manual phone calls and WhatsApp coordination with a structured, live flow:

- **Attendees** register their GPS pickup location in seconds.
- **The system** computes the most efficient pickup route automatically.
- **Drivers** follow a sorted, live-updated stop list with ETAs.
- **Attendees** see exactly when their driver will arrive — in real time.

Every organisation is a private, isolated workspace. Your data is never visible to other organisations.

## 2. Installing the App

### Android
1. Open the APK file shared with you (via WhatsApp, email, or link).
2. If prompted, tap **"Allow from this source"** or **"Install unknown apps"** — this is required for apps not from the Play Store.
3. Tap **Install**, then **Open**.

### Enable Notifications
When the app asks permission to send notifications, tap **Allow**. This is required to receive pickup alerts from your driver.

## 3. Creating Your Account

Every new user follows the same three-step flow regardless of role.

### Step 1 — Welcome Screen
When you open Rover for the first time, tap **CREATE AN ACCOUNT** on the login screen.

You will see three cards:

| Card | Choose this if… |
|---|---|
| **I'm organising events** | You are setting up and managing the organisation |
| **I'm a driver** | You will be driving members to events |
| **I'm attending an event** | You are going to events and may need a pickup |

Tap the card that matches you.

> If you arrived via a share link or QR code, the app will show a banner telling you which organisation you are joining. Your selection still applies.

### Step 2 — Register
Fill in your details:

- **Full Name** — your real name (visible to your admin and driver)
- **Email Address** — used to log in; must be valid
- **Password** — minimum 8 characters
- **Phone Number** — optional, but recommended so drivers can call you

Tap **REGISTER**.

### Step 3 — Organisation Setup
After registering you are taken to **Organisation Setup**. This is where you either create a new organisation (admins) or join an existing one (drivers and attendees). See [Section 4.1](#41-setting-up-your-organisation) or [Section 7](#7-joining-an-organisation) depending on your role.

> **Email confirmation:** Some organisations require you to confirm your email before you can proceed. Check your inbox (and spam folder) for a confirmation email, click the link, then return to Rover and sign in. You will be taken straight to Organisation Setup.

## 4. For Organisers (Admins)

You control the organisation: events, drivers, members, and invitations.

### 4.1 Setting Up Your Organisation

After registering as an organiser, you land on **Organisation Setup** with the **Create Organisation** tab selected.

Fill in:

| Field | Required? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Organisation Name | Yes | e.g. "St. Mary's Church" |
| City | No | Helps members find you via search |
| Country | No | Optional |
| Organisation Type | Yes | Church / Conference / Corporate / School / Other |

Tap **CREATE ORGANISATION**. You are immediately taken to your **Admin Dashboard**.

---

### 4.2 Creating an Event

From the Admin Dashboard, go to the **Events** tab and tap the **＋** (plus) button in the top-right corner.

Fill in the event form:

| Field | Required? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Event Name | Yes | e.g. "Sunday Morning Service" |
| Event Type | No | e.g. "Sunday Service", "Youth Group" |
| Description | No | Shown to members on the event detail page |
| Date & Time | Yes | Must be at least 1 hour in the future |
| Venue Name | Yes | Human-readable location label, e.g. "City Hall" |
| Latitude / Longitude | Yes | GPS coordinates of the venue |

Tap **CREATE EVENT**. The event appears in your Events list immediately.

> **Tip — getting GPS coordinates:** Open Google Maps, long-press the venue location, and copy the coordinates shown at the top of the screen.

---

### 4.3 Assigning a Driver

On the Events tab, find the event and tap **Assign Driver** (or the driver icon next to the event).

A list of your organisation's drivers appears. Tap the driver you want to assign. The event card will update to show the assigned driver's name.

> A driver must have already joined your organisation before they appear on this list. Share your invite link or QR code with them first (see Section 4.4).

---

### 4.4 Inviting Members (QR Code & Share Link)

Go to the **Share** tab on your Admin Dashboard.

You will see:

- **A QR Code** — members scan this with their phone camera or within Rover.
- **A share link** — tap **Share Link** to send it via WhatsApp, email, SMS, or any other app.
- **Reset Code** — if you believe your invite code has been shared with the wrong people, tap this to generate a new one. All old links and QR codes are instantly invalidated.

**How members use the link:**
- Tapping the link on a phone opens Rover directly (if installed) or takes them to the registration flow.
- They can also scan the QR code from the Join Organisation screen within the app.

---

### 4.5 Approving Join Requests

If a member could not find your QR code or link, they can search for your organisation and send a **join request**.

You will see pending requests on the **Share** tab under **Pending Join Requests**.

For each request:
- Tap **Approve** to add the member to your organisation.
- Tap **Reject** to decline.

Approved members are immediately routed to their home screen.

---

### 4.6 Managing Members

Go to the **Members** tab to see everyone in your organisation, including their role (Driver or Member) and the date they joined.

---

### 4.7 Editing or Cancelling an Event

On the **Events** tab, tap an event to expand it, then:

- **Edit** — opens the event form pre-filled with current values. Change any field and tap **SAVE**.
- **Cancel** — marks the event as cancelled. Members are not automatically notified (push notifications for event changes are on the roadmap). The event remains visible but marked as cancelled.

## 5. For Drivers

You pick up members and transport them to events.

### 5.1 Joining Your Organisation

Your admin shares a QR code or link with you. Follow the steps in [Section 7 — Joining an Organisation](#7-joining-an-organisation), selecting **I'm a driver** on the Welcome screen.

Once joined, you land on the **Driver Dashboard**.

---

### 5.2 Viewing Your Assigned Events

Your Driver Dashboard shows a list of events your admin has assigned to you, with the event name and date/time.

If the list is empty, your admin has not yet assigned any events to you. Contact them to confirm.

---

### 5.3 Starting a Route

When you are ready to begin pickups for an event:

1. Ensure your phone's GPS is turned on.
2. On your Driver Dashboard, find the event and tap **Start Route**.
3. The app reads your current GPS location and fetches all pending pickup requests for that event.
4. It calculates the most efficient pickup order automatically (nearest passenger first).
5. The pickup list appears below the event, showing each passenger's name, stop number, and estimated arrival time in minutes.

> **Re-routing:** If you need to recalculate (e.g. you have already completed some stops and want a fresh ETA), tap **Re-route** on the same event.

---

### 5.4 Using the Map

Once a route is started, a **View Map** button appears in the bottom-right corner of your screen.

Tap it to open the full-screen map, which shows:

- **A green car icon** — your current GPS position, updated in real time.
- **Numbered blue circles** — each pending or en-route pickup stop, numbered in order.
- **Grey check circles** — completed pickups.

**Map controls (top-right icons):**
- **Location arrow** — centres the map on your current position.
- **Fit screen** — zooms out to show all stops at once.

**Bottom panel:**
The lower half of the map screen is a scrollable list of all stops. Each row shows:
- Stop number, passenger name, ETA, and phone number.
- **Car icon** — tap to mark that stop as "en route" (you are heading to them now).
- **Green tick icon** — tap to mark the passenger as picked up (completed).

Tap any row in the list to centre the map on that passenger's pin.

---

### 5.5 Marking Pickups

For each stop, use the two-step status flow:

1. **On My Way** (the car icon / "On Way" button) — tap this when you are heading to that specific passenger. Their status changes to **En Route** and their pin turns blue. The passenger sees this update live on their screen.

2. **Done** (the green tick / "Done" button) — tap this once you have collected the passenger. Their pin turns grey with a check mark and they are struck through on the list.

You can also perform these actions directly from the list on your Driver Dashboard without opening the map.

## 6. For Attendees

You attend events and can request a pickup so a driver collects you.

### 6.1 Joining Your Organisation

Your admin shares a QR code or link with you. Follow the steps in [Section 7 — Joining an Organisation](#7-joining-an-organisation), selecting **I'm attending an event** on the Welcome screen.

Once joined, you land on your **Home Screen** showing upcoming events.

---

### 6.2 Browsing Events

Your Home Screen lists all active events created by your organisation's admin. Each card shows:
- Event name and type
- Date and time
- Venue name
- Whether you are subscribed

Tap any event to see its full details.

---

### 6.3 Subscribing to an Event

On the Event Detail page, tap **Subscribe**. This registers your interest in attending the event.

> You **must subscribe** before you can request a pickup. The Subscribe button must be tapped first.

To cancel your interest, tap **Unsubscribe**.

---

### 6.4 Requesting a Pickup

After subscribing, tap **Request Pickup**.

The app will ask permission to access your location. Tap **Allow**. It captures your current GPS position as your pickup point.

> **Important:** Stand at the exact location where you want to be picked up before tapping Request Pickup. The driver will navigate to these precise coordinates.

Once requested, the button changes to a **"Pickup Requested"** indicator (green, with a tick). This confirms your request is in the system. You do not need to tap anything else.

---

### 6.5 Tracking Your Driver in Real Time

On the Event Detail page, once a driver has been assigned and has started their route, a **live ETA card** appears at the bottom of the screen. It shows:

- **Your stop number** — e.g. "Pickup #3" means you are the third passenger to be collected.
- **ETA in minutes** — how long until the driver reaches you from the start of the route. This updates automatically as the route progresses.
- **Status** — one of:
  - *Pending* — driver has not yet headed to your stop.
  - *En Route* — the driver is on their way to you now.
  - *Picked Up* — completed (the driver has collected you).

**Waiting for a driver:**
If your admin has not yet assigned a driver to the event, the card shows: *"Waiting for a driver to be assigned to this event."* Check back closer to the event date.

## 7. Joining an Organisation

You need an invitation from your admin to join an organisation. There are three ways.

### 7.1 Via QR Code (Recommended)

1. Ask your admin to open the **Share** tab on their Admin Dashboard and show you the QR code.
2. In Rover, on the **Join Organisation** tab, tap **SCAN QR CODE**.
3. Point your camera at the QR code.
4. The app reads the code and joins you automatically. You are taken straight to your home screen.

> If prompted for camera permission, tap Allow.

---

### 7.2 Via Share Link

1. Your admin sends you a link (via WhatsApp, SMS, email, etc.).
2. **Option A — tap the link directly:** If Rover is installed, tapping the link opens the app automatically and pre-fills the join screen. Tap **JOIN ORGANISATION** to confirm.
3. **Option B — paste the link:** Copy the link, open Rover, go to **Join Organisation**, paste the link into the **Invite Link** field, and tap **JOIN ORGANISATION**.

The link looks like: `rover.app/join/xxxxxxxx-xxxx-xxxx-xxxx-xxxxxxxxxxxx`

---

### 7.3 Via Search (Fallback)

If you don't have a QR code or link:

1. On the **Join Organisation** tab, scroll to the bottom and tap **"Can't find your link? Search for your organisation"**.
2. Type your organisation's name or city in the search box.
3. Find your organisation in the results and tap **Request**.
4. A confirmation message appears: *"Request sent. Your administrator will be notified and can approve you from the Share tab."*
5. Wait for your admin to approve the request. You will be able to access the app once approved.

> Search only finds organisations that have been set to publicly searchable. Conference-type organisations are hidden by design and can only be joined via QR code or link.

## 8. Account Management

### Signing In
On the login screen, enter your email and password and tap **LOGIN**.

If you do not have an account yet, tap **CREATE AN ACCOUNT**.

### Forgotten Password
Tap **Forgot Password?** on the login screen. Enter your email address and tap Send. Check your inbox for a password reset link and follow the instructions.

Tap the **logout icon** (arrow pointing right) in the top-right corner of any home screen.

## 9. Troubleshooting

**"That doesn't look like a Rover invite code"**
The link or QR code you used is not valid. Ask your admin to reshare the invite link from the Share tab. Old links are invalidated when an admin resets their code.

**The app shows a loading spinner and nothing happens**
Check your internet connection. Rover requires an active connection for all features.

**My location was not captured correctly when I requested a pickup**
Cancel your request (contact your admin), move to your actual pickup spot, and request again. Make sure your phone's GPS is turned on and location permission is set to "Allow while using the app".

**I subscribed but the "Request Pickup" button is greyed out**
You must subscribe first — the button becomes active only after you have subscribed to the event. If you are subscribed and the button is still greyed out, tap the back button and re-open the event to refresh the page.

**I am not receiving notifications**
- Check that you allowed notification permission when the app first asked.
- Go to your phone's Settings → Apps → Rover → Notifications and ensure they are enabled.
- Your driver must have started the route for a notification to be sent.

**The ETA card does not appear**
The ETA card only appears once you have a pickup request AND your admin has assigned a driver AND the driver has started their route. If any of those three conditions are not met, the card will not show.

**I tapped "Start Route" but got an error**
Ensure your GPS is turned on and you granted location permission. If the error persists, close the app and try again. Make sure there are attendees with active pickup requests for that event — starting a route with no requests will return an empty list.

**The map shows the wrong location for the car icon**
The car marker reflects your phone's reported GPS position. Indoors or in areas with poor signal, GPS can be inaccurate. Move outdoors for a more accurate reading.
